Sun Whisker

At its pause, I rest in your sunlight whiskers
And the standing of green family fur
Blankets of blank blue with your winged white shores
I ask you to marry me without having to say anything
You say yes, without having to say anything 
And I hold the hand that rests inside you 
To wish for firm ground to lay our bags in
And watch me bask in, so that you may bask into me
And fall in, so that you may fall graciously 
Like a feather in a solar ring
